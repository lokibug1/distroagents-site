# AI Agents vs. Chatbots: Why Distribution Needs More Than Answers

The AI hype cycle has brought a flood of chatbot solutions to every industry. But for wholesale distribution, chatbots are fundamentally the wrong tool for the job. Here's why.

## The Chatbot Problem

Chatbots answer questions. That's it. They can tell you the status of an order, look up a price, or point you to documentation. But they can't *do* anything.

In distribution, the value isn't in answering questions, it's in taking action:

- **Placing orders** from phone calls, texts, and emails
- **Collecting receivables** through systematic outreach
- **Protecting margins** by enforcing pricing rules in real-time
- **Managing compliance** documentation automatically
- **Optimizing routes** based on real-time conditions

## What Makes an Agent Different

An AI agent is autonomous. It doesn't wait for questions. It proactively monitors, decides, and acts within defined guardrails. Think of the difference between a help desk (chatbot) and a skilled employee (agent).

### Key Differences

| Capability | Chatbot | AI Agent |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Answers questions | Yes | Yes |
| Takes action | No | Yes |
| Proactive outreach | No | Yes |
| Multi-step workflows | No | Yes |
| Learns from outcomes | Limited | Yes |
| Human escalation | Basic | Intelligent |

## Distribution-Specific Intelligence

Generic AI tools don't understand distribution. They don't know what a catch weight is, how bill-backs work, or why FOB pricing matters. DistroAgents are built from the ground up for wholesale distribution, with deep understanding of:

- **Product complexity**: SKUs, cases, pallets, catch weights, lot codes
- **Pricing structures**: Customer tiers, contract pricing, volume discounts, matrix pricing
- **Compliance requirements**: FSMA 204, HACCP, temperature monitoring, lot traceability
- **Industry workflows**: Multi-channel orders, route delivery, AR collections, credit management
